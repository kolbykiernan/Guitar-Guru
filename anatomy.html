Before learning guitar, we must learn the Musical Alphabet!

This is a little bit easier to visualize on a piano, but we'll be rockin' in no time.

Click the guitar to learn more!

This is the same alphabet that is used for guitar, piano, violin, saxophone, vocals, and most instruments. Fortunately for us, the musical alphabet uses some of the same letters as the English alphabet.

The Musical alphabet is made up of 12 notes - A, B, C, D, E, F, & G, which are seen below on the white keys, plus 5 sharps, which contain the # symbol on the black keys.

Though there are 12 notes that make up the musical alphabet, there are 88 keys on a tradtional piano. Look at the "B" key in the middle of the keyboard. Notice how we see this letter again on the far right of the keyboard. That is because the musical alphabet continues to repeat itself from the lowest keys to the highest. In the interest of learning the musical alphabet, our piano below only has 24 keys.

TYPE and HOLD the letter "c" with your keyboard. Which key did we push? TYPE and HOLD it again. What sound does it make? Now TYPE and HOLD the letter "d". Do this for each letter on the white keys until you reach the letter "b".

Notice how the further right we go, the higher, the note plays. Once, we hit "b", we are one note away from completing a "C" scale. This is when you start with a letter on the keyboard and walk up until you reach the letter again.
